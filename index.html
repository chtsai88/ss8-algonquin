Geography, Location

The Algonquian Indians near the coast of the North Carolina is the southernmost part of the entire Algonquin tribe. The subtropical coast and coastal plain is flat with many lakes and swamps.
  • Fish, clams, and oysters are plenty in the water of this subtropical region.
  • Deers, rabbits, and foxes are also plentiful.

The entire Algonquin tribe stretch as far north as Canada, and North Carolina is only a very small part of it.

Economy, Currency

  • Native to Quebec and Northern Ontario
  • Used shells called Wampum for money, not banknotes and coins
  • Fishing and Hunting
  • Traded fur with the French after contact
  • Agriculture: corn, beans, and squash
